// No, you are reading that right, no conflicts. It's focused on dripping your influence to Babylon 5.
// It also has a few combos to power it, like the For My People's and The Greater Goods (+1 B5, -1 Shadows and Vorlons).
// There are some duplicates of limited cards, but those are mostly to make sure they are found.

// Early Game: Don't show what you are doing. Bring out Susan, promote, then Proxima at ten influence
// (and hopefully another character like David or Franklin).

// Middle Game: Reveal your theme and run! Bleed over quickly, building influence to get back up
// (maybe stay at nine, if you suspect someone is packing a Point of No Return), and use Sinclair to get
// more B5 influence (Above combo, For the Common Good, Support Babylon 5, etc). Keep bringing out things
// in case on conflicts. Military can be a problem, which is why the fleets are there, as is the Negotiated Surrender.

// End Game: Run as fast as you can. Get to twenty and win, replacing your agenda if the first one is fooled with. Just run.

// Other Comments: It's pretty boring, but it wins. The card that destroys this deck is We Are Not Impressed.
// If you can't win by the time it comes out, or can't win the conflict targeting it when it first comes out
// (that's the purpose of Hidoshi), then your screwed. It's that easy to stop, but more often than not, you can
// win before anyone finds it. Good luck!
