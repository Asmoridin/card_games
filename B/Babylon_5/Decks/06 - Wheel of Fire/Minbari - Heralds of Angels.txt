// This deck keeps many options open.  Something that works well with this deck is the influence gaining with power gain from Servants of Order.  OAA is a nice card just for the power boost, but is too susceptible to We Are Not Impressed.  Another thing that works well is the Crusade card Final Destiny with Irrelevant.  I couldn't find any crusade pile that worked well with the theme, so it has no crusade pile.
// Early Game:  Get out Lennier into the inner circle.  Bring out the Agenda, and if you don't have 3 marks by the time you're ready to bring out VS, then you can buy it off of the Agenda.  Get it out, and bring out your key support.  You can use Sign and Portents in the opening hand if you think someone else is using the Shadows or Drahk, and it can get the VS with other fun cards.
// Middle Game: Get out your support, get you Trade Pacts through, and win a key conflict or two for more influence.  All the while, keep slowly raising Vorlon influence. 
// End Game: Either get the whole bit through your conflict, or switch over to OAA.  Go after We Are Not Impressed if it's played right away with Diplomacy or Military, depending on what is weakest out there.  Look innocent (like that can ever happen with tons of Vorlon marks.
// Other Comments:  I played this deck (or a version of it) at GenCon '98.  It pulled 2nd twice and 3rd once in the preliminary rounds.  However, this was due to Narn Maintain the Peace decks.
