// Well, the themes of intrigue, unrest, and capitalizing on the failings of others stand well with the card choices.

// Early Game: Because of Clark starting with two unrest, you can get Sarah in your inner circle third turn,
// bring out meddling with others, and be ready to start meddling with your 10 intrigue every turn once you
// get to ten influence to quickly bring up general unrest. Focus on people likely to have good intrigue, but
// not yet, like many Centauri decks, and wait to pick on easier targets like the Minbari after the other big
// intrigue players are full up.

// Middle Game: Once you get the table to 4 plus unrest, start playing the global enhancements to shut people down.
// By this point, you've probably been victimized by other players a few times, and hence not a target.
// This is the best time for things like Pull From Behind. Then, put out a Terror Tactics, and surprise people by
// helping the group defeat the leader.

// End Game: Go for your power conflicts, like Prey on the Weak and Attacking Pawns. TT really is cool with PotW,
// and should put you close. Then, if you are lucky, play your Not Without a Fight, and watch the pained look of the others.

// Other Comments: This deck is not a power win deck. It's a deck to have fun with and annoy other players.
// It can cause players to fear, yet not fear you winning at the same time. Fear is what you want to invoke
// in others, as while you aren't big enough to strike down well, the withholding of pain is usually worth at
// least a few discretionary points in the end :)
