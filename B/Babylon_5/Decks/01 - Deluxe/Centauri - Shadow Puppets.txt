// It's a simple influence bleeding deck really that tries not to be an easy target for conflicts.

// Early Game: It's not the quickest opening, but has all the foundation cards you need. Remember not
// to expose GiC until SZ is out for the shadow influence gain. Bring out other cards to defend yourself, 
// and try not to give yourself away as a bleeder deck.

// Middle Game: Defend yourself, and win a shadow intregue conflict or two to annoy you chief opponents.
// Use the Stasis if the oppertunity shows it self, but remember to maximize your playes to make us of SZ.
// When you feel somewhat confident, switch over to Forced Evolution. By that time, it shouldn't be a suprise,
// and you want to get it into play before someone takes it out of your hand.

// End Game: Keep bleeding over. As long as no one plays something like Point of No Return, you should have no
// problem. If you meet up with a We Are Not Impressed, go after with it using intregue or military, whichever
// the rest of the players do no have.

// Other Comments: Pretty boring deck, to tell the truth, with low player interaction. That's why I haven't played
// with it much. Can be fun sometimes, but not usually. I haven't tried it though in any sort of tourney format.
