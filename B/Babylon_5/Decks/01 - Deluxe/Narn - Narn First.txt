// Nightwatch always was an intriguing topic for deck formation, and any one who was a fan of the
// show can see the brutality they represent. However, in the B5 CCG, while there are lots of
// tempting cards, most are not overpowering enough to be worth the focus in cards and play (using
// OOPF to gather more). The one card that makes it worth it is Nightwatch Collaborators, so that every
// won OOPF conflict gives character +2 diplomacy. So this is a swarm like Narn diplomacy/strife deck
// makes even Narn Aides worth 4 diplomacy (6 in Euphrates Treaty), such that many narns become mighty.
// Council Na'Far allowing all sorts of interesting characters become Narn Nightwatch (like Gyor),
// adding to the severity of opposing this deck. 

// Early Game: It is very important that you get out the Councilor first turn, which you should be able to
// do unless you have a Centauri deck built to get to war on turn 1 or 2. Optimally, without Centauri,
// your first turn Collaborators, OOPF (choosing to gain the Conspiracy Mark), the Councilor, then choosing
// the diplomacy gain (giving a total diplomacy of 9). Then work towards getting to ten influence, bringing
// out a cheap character and promote them to give you greater speed.

// Middle Game: Bring out as many characters as you can, and conflict every turn with an OOPF. Win a conflict
// here or there, but save the bigger conflicts (like Euphrates Treaty or Rally the People (destiny mark comes
// from the Book of G'Quan)) for late in the game. 

// End Game: Win your big conflicts (late, not even everyone combined should be able to kill your ET) and
// try to win an Impasse and use your last action for Support of the Mighty, because the turn without the
// action round should let you keep your mighty characters ready for the power gain to win. 

// Other Comments: If you play in an environment with a Centauri faction in every game, you definitely
// need to put in another OOPF, and maybe even an agenda searcher. However, since we play mostly in smaller
// games, this is fine here.
