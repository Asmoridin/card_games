// Since you can have as many human cards that aren't Psi Corp as you have conspiracy marks, and you
// need the marks just to get out your big guns, I figured to use the marks and get the big human
// non-psi corp characters, like Young, Sarah, and Clark.. This deck's staying power is incredible. The
// Crusade stack works especially well with the Agents with the conspiracy marks. 

// Early Game: Stone can be in your inner circle on the third turn, and when you do send out Corp
// is Mother/Father, I usually get Infiltrate and Exploit, Kelsey, and Psi Corp Intelligence. Other
// options include Knowledge is Power, Reprogrammer, and/or Telepath Recruiters. Go for conspiracy, and hurt people.

// Middle Game: Many conflicts is happy things. Keep getting out Secret Police, as most Teeps can add
// another 5 in Intrigue at the right time, which is a major character most times. Try to keep one Conspiracy
// mark available for non-psi corp cards in case you are hit with a conflict where you need a specific
// character or fleet. You should be conflicting almost every turn, either with I&E or conflict cards.

// End Game: Support of the Mighty can give as much as 10 or 11 power, so save it back unless you know a
// factional inertia is coming your way (and remember, Secret Police on someone like a Bloodhound counts
// toward SotM). Otherwise, keep hitting the leader and building yourself up.

// Other Comments: In two games, it's split against the Steal the Throne intrigue deck, which is a
// shining accomplishment. It's hard to play right, but when you get used to it, it can be quite fearsome.
// Watch out for a HEAVY war deck, or HEAVY diplomacy, as it can often generate more influence quicker than you can.
