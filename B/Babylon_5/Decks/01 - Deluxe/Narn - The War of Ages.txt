// Yeah, I know it's kind of big. Plus, if you look closely, you'll notice that very little has
// been added since Great War. You will also see no aftermaths. This deck focuses on winning conflicts,
// and being very strong. Also, there are no contingencies, but that is mostly because I haven't seen
// anything that really fits the deck yet. Furthermore, there is no crusade pile, but that is because
// I couldn't find anything appropriate to the theme. Almost every fleet I could find is in here though
// (with exception of Vengeance fleet and some neutral fleets, which didn't strike me as being too useful.

// Early Game: Most of the fleets require Z'ha'dum to be in play, so this deck puts it out there as
// quickly as possible (the agenda allowing me to buy shadow marks if need be). Also, if will gradually
// help increase Shadow influence, which will eventually help. Get Z'ha'dum out, and start pumping out
// fleets. Also, I often bring Ko'Dath out second turn (gaining influence first turn) because our play
// area is too fond of Airlock Mishap.

// Middle Game: If you see a good opening, or other military is slow, take advantage of it. Otherwise,
// just defend yourself, and put out more fleets and prepare to go to war with others. If there is no war,
// keep the fleets going, as it may help with a sudden power boost of Never Again (possible 12 power, optimally).

// End Game: Go for one of the power agendas when you want to go for the win, and keep jamming conflicts
// down the throats of the other players. They might unify, but Forced Evolution with ZA will put you
// over the top when the time comes.

// Other Comments: Playing against a Drahk deck, this deck nearly lost it because of the Shadow Mark
// stealer (Master of Darkness). That hurts lots, and if you know you're playing them, have Power Play
// ready to dump out enough marks to get your FE out. Otherwise, this deck is just plain nasty.
