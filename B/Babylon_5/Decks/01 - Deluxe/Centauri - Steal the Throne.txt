// This deck makes Refa a VERY bad man, and the enhancements just keep piling it. The Emperor with
// both Covert Allies and Power Posturing is truly scary.

// Early Game: Usually, you go first, so PASS your first action, and then play the Mishap and then
// the Agent. Turn two, promote, and bring out the Agenda. You'll be up and doing things turn 5.
// Not bad, and the intrigue of 10 is very nice that early.

// Middle Game: There are many intrigue conflicts in this deck, so use them and build a following.
// Keep building up Refa as much as possible. Make him a monster, and either spearhead your attacks
// with him, or just keep him around as a huge threat to others who might interfere.

// End Game: Rise of the Republic, or just bully your way to victory. Not subtle, but very strong.

// Other Comments: Military can be a major pain to this deck. This has been addressed a bit with
// many fleets added, and now it should keep you from spinning out of control, and squeak to victory
// before you become a bloody lump. He's the one likely to blink first.
