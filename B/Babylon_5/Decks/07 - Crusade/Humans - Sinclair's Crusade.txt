// Some things will change with this deck when I finish getting another copy of the Ivanova line,
// more Wells, and a spare Ellison to finish assembling the Excalibur crew. Some of the interesting
// decisions in this deck include keeping the ISA events in the deck, not opening hand. Partially this is
// because our environment doesn't support the ISA much. I'll burn cards to get ISA cards from out of play
// if it goes through. Particularly this includes ISA president, and often the Fleets or But We're Your Allies.

// Early Game: It’s a pretty straightforward opening, and early, all you want is to pump out characters and build your diplomacy.

// Middle Game: This is when I usually start putting out fleets, more characters (very rarely do I not have
// one to play), and really start inflating B5 influence. The three New Teams and three Goods and Services
// really help the inner circle grow to a solid level (especially factoring in Chief Allan and Garabaldi CEO).
// Make friends here with things like ISA, Trade Pacts (and Good Trade with ISA), Something in the Air, etc.

// End Game: Use PiOT if you can get a power boost, but otherwise just plow through with conflicts. Straightforward again.

// Other Comments: I wanted a Sinclair deck since our area seemed Sheridan and Clark crazy. It has done surprisingly
// well, particularly because I can almost always out character opponents, plus with Human diplomacy with THOP, it is very nasty.
