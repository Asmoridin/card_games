// The focus here was making a strong, efficient military deck, and one of the greatest strengths of the
// humans is lots of characters with leadership (hence using War College). That, adding in that there are
// also a few REALLY good leaders (Sheridan, Ivanova, and Lockley all spring to mind), making single fleet
// combat attractive, which is unusually with most human fleet military numbers. I chose not to fully evolve
// Sheridan because of the length of time required usually meant that Sheridan usually didn't get evolved
// most games anyway.

// Early Game: Turn 1 - RG, Susan. 
// Turn 2 promote, Onslaught (often face down, which worries some since human decks can be good for so much).
// At 10 with 7 applicable by turn 5. Decently fast, and with a good draw, you might be ready to try a conflict by turn 6.

// Middle Game: Build up fleets and personnel as quickly and efficiently as possible, maintaining a fleet for
// every leader whenever possible. Pick on those that don't look adequately prepared for military, and
// keep out enough other skills to avoid looking weak yourself. If you get Hello Old Friend, get M. Garibaldi, CEO,
// and use him to improve Ivanova fast, because her power is often enough to make people look away from you (no one
// wants to risk making you stronger if you play player politics well).

// End Game: Higher Calling is your closing agenda, but it will only be enough for 2-3 power, so you need to keep
// pressing military advantage. If a stronger military is playing as well, spend time playing hero to gain player
// support, squash the infidel, and then go for military superiority. This deck stream-rolls well with Onslaught to
// get fleets, but it is harder to come back when behind. 

// Other Comments: This deck originally was Evolving a Better Leader, when I realized we had lots of people playing
// humans, and none of them playing military. I always like the spice of adding different things to keep the environment fresh :)
